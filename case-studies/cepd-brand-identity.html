CEPD Brand Identity

Two complete brand systems: the one they approved, and the one they asked for instead.

CEPD needed an identity. I pitched mitosis: cells dividing, knowledge replicating, education that never stops. The AVP of External Relations approved it. The portfolio's stakeholders didn't get it and killed it. So I built them a second complete system in a safer register and shipped every piece of it. This page shows both.

01 — THE CONCEPTMitosis

Mitosis is what continuing education looks like under a microscope: a cell divides, knowledge replicates, the profession grows. I built it into a visual language with a custom cell motif, a pattern library in three colourways, a colour of its own (CEPD Lakeside Teal, picked for the lakes and argued for its contrast ratios), and a 17-slide brand guide.

02 — THE VERDICTApproved upstairs, killed in the room

The AVP saw university-wide potential and said so. The CEPD stakeholders saw circles they didn't understand. That's the job sometimes. The concept isn't dead; the university just hasn't organized around it yet.

03 — THE FALLBACKThe system that shipped

Rejection wasn't an excuse to ship nothing. The second system covers everything the portfolio actually needed: save-the-date banners through five revisions, six video-conference backgrounds, microcredential flyers, eight social templates, a reusable PowerPoint template, and a conference kit for Northern Constellations.

04 — THE NEWSLETTERCoded, not mocked

The newsletter isn't a picture of an email. It's HTML I wrote by hand, table layout and all, because email clients don't care how good your Figma file looks.

TL;DR

  • The full CEPD template suite shipped and is in use.
  • The mitosis concept holds AVP-level approval for future university-wide work.
  • Nearly every deliverable went through three or more stakeholder revision rounds.
